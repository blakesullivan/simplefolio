Fair Dinkum Sheds

Created in VB.Net in Visual Studio 2019, Fair Dinkum Sheds' MultSuite is a suite of programs with the sole purpose of creating safe, strong, and attractive steel sheds and carports.
The suite consists of:
MultiBuild: The flagship program of the suite. It is used to design and engineer steel sheds. It has a engineering system built in to it so that each shed is designed to be the cost effective while being strong enough to withstand what is expected of it according to the regulations.
MultiPort: A supplimentary program similar in design to MultiBuild but focusing on steel carports. It has a few specific roofing designs and uses a database of profiles to determine what material to use.
MultiBOM: The program used to check and modify the Bill of Materials generated by MultiBuild and MultiPort and send it through to be ordered.
MultiPrice: This program displays the different price files that the programs use, giving the users the ability to modify items from standard and take a copy of the details to be inserted into the BOM.
MultiSuite Launcher: This multi-feature program has a few basic, but important, functions. It runs the programs, it updates them, it displays promotions, and tracks orders.

In addition there is a customer facing app:
Fair Dinkum Sheds Shed Designer: A customer focused Unity 3D app was created to support MultiSuite, making it easier for customers to design the shed they want and request a quote from a Distributor. I did not personally work on this app.
I have linked to the FDS Shed Designer app, as no information is made public about MultiSuite, but the app does show a simpler process of creating a structure in MultiSuite.

Shed Boss

Shed Boss App:
Working in a two person team I designed and implemented the User Interface, among other parts, for this Unity 3D app.
Unity 3D* was used as it allowed us to make the app cross-platform much easier.
It is currently available on Android, iOS, and webGL.
The Shed Boss App is used by customers to create the steel shed they want to purchase. Once they have finished their shed, if they are using the mobile app, they can view the shed in AR to see how it will fit in the desired position.
Once they have finalised their shed they can then send it off for a quote from one of the Shed Boss outlets across Australia, who themselves can then view the shed in the App or import it into the ShedBuilder custom software they use to quote the shed.

*Note: This was created in Unity 3D v4.-, before the implementation of the UnityGUI.
